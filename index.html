Glossary of Assessment Tools

FIM (Functional Independence Measure)

The FIM is a standardized tool used to assess the level of assistance required by a person to perform basic life activities. Scores range from 1 to 7, with higher scores indicating greater independence.

FIM Scoring Guide:

7 Complete Independence - Person performs task safely, with no modifications, assistive devices, or aids, and within reasonable time
6 Modified Independence - Person requires assistive device, more than reasonable time, or safety considerations
5 Supervision/Setup - Person requires supervision, cueing, or setup
4 Minimal Assistance - Person performs 75% or more of task
3 Moderate Assistance - Person performs 50-74% of task
2 Maximal Assistance - Person performs 25-49% of task
1 Total Assistance - Person performs less than 25% of task or task cannot be performed safely

COPM (Canadian Occupational Performance Measure)

The COPM is a client-centered assessment tool designed to measure a person's self-perception of their performance and satisfaction with daily activities. Scores range from 1 to 10.

COPM Performance Scale (1-10):

1 = Not able to perform the activity at all
10 = Able to perform the activity extremely well

COPM Satisfaction Scale (1-10):

1 = Not satisfied at all with performance
10 = Extremely satisfied with performance
